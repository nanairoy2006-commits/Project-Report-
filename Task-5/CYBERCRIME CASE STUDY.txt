CYBERCRIME CASE STUDY

Topic: Phishing

Phishing is a type of cybercrime where attackers trick users into providing sensitive information such as passwords, OTPs, or bank details. It usually occurs through fake emails, messages, or websites that appear legitimate.

In a typical phishing attack, the victim receives an email pretending to be from a bank or trusted service. The email contains a link that redirects to a fake website. The victim enters login credentials, which are then stolen by the attacker.

Students and online users are common targets because they frequently use digital platforms. The consequences include financial loss, identity theft, and unauthorized account access.

Phishing attacks are dangerous because they exploit human trust rather than technical vulnerabilities. Awareness and caution are essential to prevent such attacks.
